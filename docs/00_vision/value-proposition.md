# DomusMind — Value Proposition

## Core Statement

DomusMind is the system that makes the operational state of a household visible, shared, and reliable — so nothing depends on one person’s memory.

---

## Target

Households where coordination is already breaking down.

Specifically:

- couples managing shared responsibilities
- families with children
- households where one person carries most of the mental load

---

## The Problem

Household coordination is invisible, fragmented, and fragile.

In most homes:

- plans live in calendars
- tasks live in lists
- responsibilities are implied
- reminders happen in chat or in someone’s head

This creates a hidden system where:

- one person tracks everything
- others depend on being told
- things are forgotten or duplicated
- coordination fails silently until it becomes conflict

The real problem is not organization.

The problem is that **the system of the household does not exist as a shared, external reality**.

---

## Emotional Reality

This leads to:

- constant background stress
- cognitive overload
- feeling of unfairness
- dependence on one person to “keep everything running”

---

## The Shift

DomusMind turns implicit coordination into explicit structure.

Instead of:

- remembering
- reminding
- checking multiple tools

The household operates on a shared system where:

- what is happening is visible
- who is responsible is clear
- what needs attention is explicit

The system holds the state.

Not a person.

---

## The Outcome

With DomusMind:

- nothing depends on memory
- everyone sees the same reality
- responsibilities are transparent
- coordination becomes predictable
- conflicts are reduced before they happen

The result is not better organization.

The result is a household that runs with less effort and less tension.

---

## How It Works (Mechanism)

DomusMind is built on a simple but strict model:

### 1. Time is explicit
Everything that happens is placed in time.

- plans are visible
- schedules are shared
- participants are clear

### 2. Work is explicit
Everything that must be done is defined.

- tasks are concrete
- routines are consistent
- completion is visible

### 3. Responsibility is explicit
Ownership is structured, not implied.

- each domain has an owner
- accountability is visible
- no ambiguity about who is responsible

### 4. Everything converges into one timeline

The household is not split across tools.

It is represented as a single, continuous flow of activity:

- plans
- tasks
- routines
- reminders

All visible in one place.

---

## Differentiation

DomusMind is not:

- a shared calendar
- a task manager
- a family organizer

Those tools manage fragments.

DomusMind models the **entire operational state of the household**.

Key differences:

- unified timeline instead of multiple tools
- explicit responsibility model instead of implicit ownership
- separation between time (plans) and execution (tasks)
- household-first perspective instead of individual productivity

The system that removes the need to keep your household in your head.

### Variant B — Fairness
Make responsibility visible, so the load is shared, not assumed.

### Variant C — Coordination
Know what’s happening, who’s responsible, and what matters — at a glance.

### Variant D — Reliability
Turn your household into a system that doesn’t rely on memory.

### Variant E — Outcome
A household that runs smoothly, without constant reminders.

---

## Truth Constraint

DomusMind does not promise automation or intelligence.

It provides:

- clarity
- structure
- shared visibility

From that, coordination improves naturally.
