# DomusMind - Product Strategy

This document is the canonical product-definition file for DomusMind.

It defines the product truth that strategy, experience, and public messaging must share.

---

# Mission

DomusMind exists to remove the invisible mental load of running a household.

It turns scattered plans, routines, tasks, areas, lists, and reminders into one shared household system so the home no longer depends on one person remembering everything.

The product goal is not better personal productivity.

The product goal is a household that runs with more clarity, less friction, and less dependence on memory.

---

# Vision

DomusMind aims to become the trusted shared system for household life.

The long-term direction is a home where:

- responsibilities are visible
- plans and work are legible in one place
- overloaded days are visible early
- coordination improves before conflict appears
- the household depends on shared structure instead of one person's memory

Over time, the system should support anticipation and useful suggestions, but that intelligence must grow from clear household structure rather than replace it.

---

# Category

DomusMind is a household coordination system.

It is household-first, not person-first.

It models the household as a living system made of people, time, responsibilities, and ongoing work.

---

# Positioning

For couples and families who are tired of carrying household coordination in their heads, DomusMind provides one shared system that makes plans, routines, tasks, lists, and areas visible together.

Unlike fragmented tools, DomusMind is built around the real operating reality of home life:

- visible ownership by area
- recurring routines
- household schedules
- daily and weekly coordination
- the constant question of what matters today

---

# Value Proposition

DomusMind helps a household stop depending on one person's memory.

It brings plans, routines, tasks, lists, reminders, and areas into one shared household picture so everyone can understand:

- what is happening
- what needs attention
- who is responsible
- what happens next

The result is less reminder work, clearer ownership, earlier coordination, and a home that runs more smoothly.

---

# Target Audience

## Primary Audience

- couples and families where one person carries most of the household mental load

## Secondary Audiences

- parents coordinating school, activities, appointments, and routines
- couples trying to share household responsibilities more fairly
- households tired of reminders, chats, and repeated negotiation
- early adopters interested in better systems for home life

## Not the Target

- people looking for a generic productivity app
- users who only need a personal calendar
- users who only want a simple list tool

---

# Pains

Household coordination usually breaks down because the system is implicit.

Common pains include:

- one person keeps track of everything
- others depend on being told what matters
- plans are split across calendars, chats, and memory
- recurring work keeps being renegotiated
- ownership is assumed instead of visible
- busy days become obvious too late
- things are forgotten until they become stress or conflict

The core problem is not lack of effort.

The core problem is lack of shared visibility and shared household memory.

---

# Gains

DomusMind should create gains the household can feel quickly:

- nothing important depends on one person's memory
- everyone can see what matters today
- ownership becomes visible instead of implied
- routine work stops becoming repeated negotiation
- weekly coordination becomes easier to understand
- the household reacts less and anticipates more
- the home runs with less friction and less tension

---

# Differentiators

DomusMind is differentiated by a small set of product truths:

- The household is treated as a system, not a pile of disconnected tasks.
- State matters more than task lists. The product stores household facts that make work and coordination legible.
- Responsibility must be explicit. Ownership should be visible, not assumed.
- Time and work are visible together. Plans and tasks converge into one household picture.
- The product is timeline-first and today-first. The main question is what matters now, not what features exist.
- Capture must stay easier than remembering, or adoption fails.
- Anticipation matters more than reaction. The system should help the household see pressure before it turns into friction.
- Lists are reusable grouped memory, not task tracking. They reduce mental load by persisting what the household needs to remember — without collapsing into a task manager or a calendar.

---

# Non-Goals

DomusMind is not:

- a generic task manager
- a traditional calendar application
- a simple household-work tracker in isolation
- a budget tool
- a grocery app by itself
- a family chat replacement
- a dashboard full of abstract metrics

DomusMind also does not promise magic automation.

The product promise is shared clarity, visible responsibility, and a better operating model for the household.

# Meal Planning Addition

Meal planning is an extension of the existing household coordination system, not a separate product. It should:

- Integrate seamlessly with existing Lists and Agenda surfaces
- Follow the same family-first, household-level approach as other features
- Avoid recipe database complexity or social features
- Support weekly reuse patterns through templates and history
- Generate shopping lists automatically from meal plans

---

# Language Rules

Product and public language must stay close to lived household reality.

Prefer:

- household
- people
- plans
- routines
- tasks
- lists
- areas
- today
- what matters
- what needs attention
- who owns what

Avoid in user-facing product definition and public copy:

- operational state
- bounded context
- domain model
- coordination infrastructure
- execution layer
- accountability model
- aggregate
- projection

When internal model terms and household language differ, household-facing communication should use the household term.

Core translations:

| Internal Model | Household Language |
| -------------- | ------------------ |
| Family | Household |
| Member | Person |
| Event | Plan |
| Responsibility | Area |

Task stays Task by design because it already matches natural household language.
