# DomusMind - Public Site

This document is the canonical public-site file for DomusMind.

It is the only product document that should speak explicitly in website, copy, conversion, and SEO terms.

It translates the product definition in [strategy.md](strategy.md) into public-site rules.

It must not redefine the product.

---

# Homepage Structure

The homepage should follow a fixed narrative order:

1. Hero
2. Reality
3. Breakdown
4. The Shift
5. What You See
6. How It Works
7. What Exists Today
8. Early Access / Support the Project

Each section must reinforce one invariant:

> The house stops depending on one person's memory.

Section intent:

- Hero: immediate recognition of the problem and the system shift.
- Reality: make the visitor feel this is exactly their household.
- Breakdown: explain why fragmented tools fail.
- The Shift: introduce DomusMind as the shared household system.
- What You See: make the product visible through the Today view.
- How It Works: explain the operating flow without technical language.
- What Exists Today: stay inside real V1 scope.
- Early Access / Support the Project: support testers, followers, and early supporters without breaking the main household conversion path.

---

# Homepage Content Blocks

The homepage should be composable from a small set of concrete blocks:

- Headline: pain-first and household-specific.
- Support line: describe the shared-system shift in plain language.
- 3 proof bullets: show what the household gets immediately.
- CTA pair: one primary action and one secondary action.
- Screenshot caption rules: each image proves one household outcome only.

Recommended proof bullets:

- See what matters today in one shared view.
- Make responsibility visible without repeated reminders.
- Understand busy days before they become friction.

Recommended CTA pair:

- Primary: Start your household
- Secondary: See how it works

Screenshot caption rules:

- one caption, one idea
- describe the household outcome, not the UI widget
- prefer Today, Week, routines, and shared visibility examples
- avoid feature inventory language

# Hero Rules

The hero must use three lines of meaning:

- reality
- system shift
- outcome

Recommended default hero:

Headline:

**Stop carrying your household in your head.**

Support line:

DomusMind brings plans, routines, tasks, lists, and areas into one shared place, so everyone can see what matters, what needs doing, and who owns what.

Hero rules:

- lead with the lived problem before the solution label
- keep language concrete and household-facing
- make the shared-system shift explicit
- avoid abstract framing or software language
- show the outcome in practical terms, not aspiration alone

Recommended screenshot narrative:

# Copy Guardrails

Public copy must:

- use concrete household situations
- use short, human language
- lead with pain and outcome
- make the reader feel seen quickly
- show the product through daily examples
- stay understandable within seconds
- keep claims aligned with real product behavior

Public copy must not:

- explain the architecture
- sound like documentation
- sound like B2B SaaS software
- promise AI magic the product cannot deliver
- use broad lifestyle language such as organize your life
- rely on negation without a positive product definition
- invent features outside the real system scope

Approved public-language anchors include:

- household
- people
- plans
- routines
- tasks
- lists
- areas
- today
- who owns what
- what matters
- what needs attention

Avoid architecture-facing wording in public copy.

Every public sentence should pass this test:

1. Would a tired parent or overloaded partner understand it immediately?
2. Does it sound like home life rather than software design?
3. Does it make the pain visible?
4. Does it make the outcome desirable?
5. Can the product actually deliver it?
