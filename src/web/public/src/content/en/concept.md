## What a household really is

A household is not a list of tasks.

It is a group of people sharing a space and a life.
Each person has things they need to do, places they need to be, and responsibilities they hold.
Those things overlap, depend on each other, and change over time.

That is a system.

## Why tasks are not the problem

If Sarah forgets to buy milk, that is a task.
But the reason Sarah is the one who noticed the milk was running out - that is not a task.
That is a pattern. A responsibility. A structural role she has taken on.

No task app captures that.

## What is actually happening

At any moment, a household has a state.

Someone needs to be at school pickup at 15:30.
There is a parents meeting Thursday that needs preparation.
The family agreed Liam handles the bins on Monday.
Max has not been walked today.

This state is real. It drives what needs to happen.
Right now, it exists only in one person's head.

## What breaks

When one person holds the full picture, the house depends on them.

If they forget - something fails.
If they are sick - nothing moves.
If they leave - knowledge disappears.

This is fragility by design. The system has a single point of failure.

## A full scenario

**The Smith Family**

Sarah holds in her head:

- Liam has football practice Tuesday at 17:30
- School meeting Thursday morning - she needs to leave by 08:45
- Milk and bread needed by Wednesday
- Michael said he would handle the car service - but she still checks
- Max (dog) needs his flea treatment this week

The rest of the family moves through the week.
Sarah adjusts around everyone.

Each person sees what is happening.
Nobody has to hold it all alone.

## What changes

DomusMind replaces the single mental model with a shared one.

- Responsibilities are named and assigned
- Events are on a shared calendar, connected to real people
- Tasks belong to the person who owns them
- Routines run on schedule, visible to everyone

The house does not depend on one person's memory.
