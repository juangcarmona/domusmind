## Invisible cognitive load is real

Household operations include schedules, school logistics, meals, documents, contracts, maintenance, finances, and care tasks. Much of this remains undocumented and concentrated in one person.

## Mental labor is uneven by default

When memory lives in one person’s head, workload distribution is invisible. The result is stress, imbalance, and brittle coordination.

## Households lack infrastructure

Teams and companies use operational systems. Households typically do not. Families need infrastructure designed for shared clarity, continuity, and anticipation.

## Shared operational memory

DomusMind models people, responsibilities, timeline, routines, assets, documents, properties, inventory, pets, and logistics as durable household state.

## The vision

DomusMind is open platform infrastructure for family operations: API-first, domain-centric, event-driven, and self-hostable. It evolves from coordination to anticipation and automation.
