## The reality

In most households, one person carries the house in their head.

Sarah knows what time the football practice ends.
She knows the school meeting is Thursday.
She knows the milk is running out.
She knows the vet appointment is next week.
She knows Liam is anxious about the exam.

Nobody asked her to hold all of this.
It just happened.

The house works because Sarah keeps track of everything.

## The hidden work

Sarah is not just doing tasks.

She is remembering.
She is anticipating what will be needed.
She is coordinating between people who do not know what the others need.
She is reminding, following up, and filling the gaps.

This work does not appear on any list.
It is not visible.
It is not shared.

## The imbalance

When Sarah stops keeping track of everything, things fall through.

Not because the others are careless.
But because the others do not carry the full picture.

Remembering everything for everyone falls to one person. That is the problem.

It is not a personal failing. It is a structural gap.

## Why tools fail

A task app shows a list.
A calendar shows time.

Neither shows who is responsible.
Neither shows what the household needs to know to keep running.
Neither models the house as a system.

So the coordination still falls to one person.
The tools made the tasks visible. They left the system invisible.

## The missing system

A household is a system.

It has people, roles, and responsibilities.
It has recurring patterns - routines, events, seasonal obligations.
It has a state - what is happening, what is coming, what needs attention.

Right now, that state lives in one person's head.

It should be shared.

## The shift

DomusMind makes household state visible to everyone in the family.

Who is responsible for each area.
What is happening this week.
What tasks exist and who owns them.
What routines keep things running.

Each person sees what the house needs.
No one person carries it all alone.

## The vision

DomusMind is an open system built by people who believe the house should not depend on one person's memory.

It is self-hostable.
The data belongs to the family.
The system can be inspected, extended, and contributed to.

The house can work for everyone.
